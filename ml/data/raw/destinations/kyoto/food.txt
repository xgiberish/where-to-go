Kyoto cuisine, known as Kyo-ryori, emphasizes seasonal ingredients, delicate presentation, and umami-forward flavors. Kaiseki (multi-course haute cuisine) originated in Kyoto and can be experienced at both Michelin-starred restaurants and affordable lunch sets. Tofu plays a central role in the local diet; yudofu (hot tofu) served in the Arashiyama district is a must-try.

The Nishiki Market, often called Kyoto's Kitchen, runs for five blocks and sells pickled vegetables, fresh tofu, skewered grilled foods, matcha sweets, and sake. Street food highlights include mitarashi dango (sweet rice dumplings), yatsuhashi (cinnamon rice cakes), and hot matcha drinks.
