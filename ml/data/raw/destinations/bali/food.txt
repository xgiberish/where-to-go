Balinese cuisine is rich in flavor and deeply tied to the island's Hindu culture. Signature dishes include Babi Guling (suckling pig), Bebek Betutu (slow-cooked duck), Lawar (minced meat with vegetables and coconut), and Nasi Campur (mixed rice plate with small portions of various dishes).

Ubud is the food capital of Bali, offering everything from traditional warungs (local eateries) to world-class farm-to-table restaurants. The island also has a thriving vegan and raw food scene, particularly around Ubud and Canggu. Fresh tropical fruits like mangosteen, rambutan, and salak (snake fruit) are widely available and affordable.
